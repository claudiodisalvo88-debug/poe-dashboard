# RQ-023_FORMULA_CANDIDATE_RESEARCH_OPENING_BOUNDARY

## Title

Formula candidate research opening boundary

## Question

When may PoE open future research on reputation formula candidates without yet defining formula candidates, final formula, formula syntax, weights, coefficients, scalar score, ranking, leaderboard, incentives, tokens, payout, economic allocation or implementation?

## Scope

* Define formula candidate research opening boundary
* Define boundary between admissible formula constraints, opening boundary, future formula candidates and future formula
* Define opening conditions
* Define blocking conditions
* Define failure outcomes
* Define anti-leakage rules
* Preserve non-economic, non-implementative, non-computational status

* No formula candidates

## Definition

PoE may open formula-candidate research only as a research-only phase, after upstream artifacts are repository-confirmed, PM-reviewed, current, bounded, source-referenced, anti-manipulation-safe and explicitly separated from formula syntax, weights, coefficients, score, ranking, incentive, token, payout, economic allocation and implementation.

Formula candidate research opening boundary is a non-economic, non-implementative and non-computational protocol boundary that defines when PoE may open a future research-only phase on possible formula candidates without proposing formula candidates or formula mechanics.

This boundary does not define formula candidates, final formula, formula syntax, weights, coefficients, normalization, scalar score, total score, ranking, leaderboard, incentives, rewards, tokens, token rights, token issuance, payout, economic allocation, economic scoring, API, database, dashboard, poe.db, implementation, cross-IE formula synthesis, scalar formula research or ranking / incentive / token / payout research.

## Validated decision

PD-023 - VALIDATED

## Boundary distinctions

* Admissible formula constraints define what future formula research must preserve and must not violate.
* Formula candidate research opening boundary decides when research-only work may open.
* Future formula candidates are later proposed structures. They remain out of scope for RQ-023.
* Future reputation formula is a later validated rule. It remains out of scope for RQ-023.

Binding distinction:

* constraints restrict
* opening boundary authorizes research-only opening
* candidates propose
* formula calculates

## Opening conditions

1. Repository state confirms RQ-022 closed and PD-022 validated: future research may open only after the upstream repository state is formally consolidated.
2. PM review explicitly approves research-only opening: there must be an explicit PM authorization for opening the next phase.
3. Upstream artifacts are complete and validated: object, readiness, admissibility and related upstream documents must already be present and validated.
4. IE primitive separation is preserved: the opening phase must inherit explicit separation across IE primitives.
5. Boundary integrity is preserved: research may open only if node, asset, site or other validated boundaries remain intact.
6. Time-window integrity is preserved: the relevant temporal framing must remain explicit and coherent.
7. Evidence lineage is intact: source references from upstream evidence must still be traceable.
8. Stale / conflict / capped / conditional states are handled: limited or problematic evidence states must remain visible and bounded.
9. Anti-manipulation controls are active: previously validated anti-gaming constraints must remain active at the opening stage.
10. No cross-IE netting: no opening is allowed if cross-IE compensation is silently assumed.
11. No hidden weights: the opening phase cannot embed coefficients or relative importance.
12. No hidden score: the opening phase cannot compress the protocol state into a scalar.
13. No hidden ranking: the opening phase cannot imply ordinal comparison.
14. No hidden incentive: the opening phase cannot imply reward or benefit logic.
15. No hidden token right: the opening phase cannot imply token issuance, token entitlement or token-backed claims.
16. No economic allocation: the opening phase cannot imply allocation, payout or economic scoring.
17. No implementation binding: the opening phase cannot hard-bind future research to code, API, database or dashboard structures.
18. Explicit research-only status: the opening phase must be declared as research-only and not as formula design or implementation.

## Blocking conditions

* stale repository state: the repository is not current enough to authorize opening.
* RQ-022 not closed: the previous research boundary is not formally closed.
* PD-022 not validated: admissibility constraints are not formally validated.
* missing PM review: no explicit PM review authorizes the opening.
* incomplete upstream artifacts: required upstream documents are missing or incomplete.
* missing source references: the upstream evidence chain cannot be traced.
* incomplete boundary: boundary definitions are missing or ambiguous.
* incompatible time windows: temporal scopes are mixed in a way that breaks coherence.
* stale evidence not marked: stale inputs are being treated as current without marking.
* unresolved conflict evidence: known conflicts remain unresolved or unbounded.
* capped / conditional states not separated: limited evidence states are being mixed into ordinary states.
* unmanaged manipulation risk: gaming or manipulation risk remains materially open.
* cross-IE netting risk: research opening would silently rely on cross-IE compensation.
* hidden weights risk: implicit weighting logic is already leaking into the opening phase.
* hidden score risk: a scalar signal is being introduced too early.
* hidden ranking risk: the opening phase is already ordering nodes or outcomes.
* incentive leakage: reward logic is leaking into the research opening.
* token leakage: token logic or token-backed claims are leaking into the research opening.
* economic leakage: payout, allocation or economic value logic is leaking into the research opening.
* implementation pressure: code, API, database or dashboard requirements are being used to drive protocol content.
* request for formula syntax, coefficients, score or ranking: the phase is being pushed beyond opening conditions into formula design.

## Failure outcomes

* formula-research-opening-blocked: the opening phase cannot start because one or more core conditions are missing.
* formula-research-opening-conditional: the phase may open only under explicit and bounded conditions.
* formula-research-opening-partial: only a limited part of the upstream structure is ready for research opening.
* formula-research-opening-capped: the phase may open only with explicit bounded limits due to residual uncertainty.
* formula-research-opening-stale-blocked: stale-state problems block the opening.
* formula-research-opening-conflict-blocked: unresolved conflicts block the opening.
* formula-research-opening-manipulation-blocked: manipulation risk blocks the opening.
* formula-research-opening-economic-leakage-blocked: economic leakage blocks the opening.
* formula-research-opening-implementation-blocked: implementation pressure blocks the opening.
* formula-research-opening-approved: the protocol may open a future research-only phase, without proposing formula candidates.

## Anti-leakage rules

* no formula by discussion: discussion about opening cannot become hidden formula design.
* no hidden formula syntax: the opening phase cannot embed symbolic or structural formula syntax.
* no hidden formula candidates: the opening phase cannot smuggle candidate formulas into the boundary.
* no hidden weights: the opening phase cannot embed coefficients or weighting logic.
* no hidden score: the opening phase cannot collapse into a scalar score.
* no hidden ranking: the opening phase cannot create ordinal ordering.
* no hidden leaderboard: the opening phase cannot create comparative public standings.
* no hidden incentive: the opening phase cannot imply reward logic.
* no hidden token right: the opening phase cannot imply token claims or entitlement.
* no hidden payout: the opening phase cannot imply payout logic.
* no hidden economic allocation: the opening phase cannot imply distribution or allocation of economic value.
* no premature implementation: the opening phase cannot be justified by implementation needs.
* no false precision: the opening phase cannot create unjustified precision claims.
* no gaming target creation: the opening phase cannot create obvious optimization targets before formula research is authorized.
* no cross-IE collapse: the opening phase cannot silently merge distinct IE dimensions into one evaluative track.
